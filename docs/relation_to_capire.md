# Relation to CAPIRE

CAPIRE (Curricular Architecture for Professional Integrated Research Education) is the
agent-based student trajectory simulation system that operates alongside AlphaPath.

---

## Conceptual Relationship

AlphaPath and CAPIRE address complementary questions:

| AlphaPath | CAPIRE |
|---|---|
| What architectures are structurally viable? | How do students traverse an architecture? |
| Which candidate ranks highest by structural metrics? | What outcomes emerge under student heterogeneity? |
| What is the institutional packaging cost? | What is the social and temporal cost to students? |
| Which design is robust under metric perturbation? | Which design is robust under population heterogeneity? |

---

## CAPIRE Archetypes

CAPIRE models student populations through trajectory archetypes: parameterised
representations of different student profiles, progression styles, and risk patterns.

These archetypes are derived from empirical research on student trajectories and provide
the heterogeneous agent population for simulation.

The archetype catalogue is maintained in the private AlphaPath core repository.

---

## Integration in the Evaluation Stack

CAPIRE-based simulation is integrated into Stage 4 (Simulation- and Uncertainty-Aware
Evaluation) of the AlphaPath evaluation stack.

Candidates that survive structural and institutional evaluation are subsequently compared
under simulated student populations to assess transition support coverage and outcome
stability.
