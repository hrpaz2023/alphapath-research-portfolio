# Campaign Series

AlphaPath was evaluated through a structured sequence of campaign-based experiments.
Each campaign targeted a specific research question about curriculum architecture
redesign. This document summarises the campaigns at the level of research intent.

Full campaign bundles, configurations, and outputs remain in the private core repository.

---

## Campaign 001 — Baseline vs. Candidate Universe

**Research question:** How does the incumbent 2005 Civil Engineering curriculum compare
to the space of structurally generated alternative architectures?

Establishes the baseline metrics and the first competitive candidate set.

---

## Campaign 002 — Uncertainty Stress and Winner Stability

**Research question:** Are apparent performance leaders stable under uncertainty?

Applies uncertainty-aware evaluation to separate candidates whose apparent leadership
is fragile from those who lead robustly across conditions.

---

## Campaign 003 — Local Synchronisation Relief

**Research question:** Can targeted synchronisation adjustments near the baseline
resolve the identified structural constraint?

Tests the hypothesis that minor scheduling interventions produce meaningful
improvement. Develops evidence for the reparation limit concept.

---

## Campaign 004 — Prerequisite Surgery

**Research question:** Can selective removal or restructuring of early prerequisites
relieve the downstream blockage identified in the structural diagnosis?

Tests the hypothesis that targeted prerequisite modification is sufficient.
Extends the reparation limit analysis.

---

## Campaign 005 — Packaging Elasticity

**Research question:** Can institutional packaging flexibility absorb the structural
constraint without changing the underlying PCU graph?

Tests the range of packaging-level intervention within fixed structural designs.

---

## Campaign 006 — Subject Aggregation

**Research question:** Can subject aggregation strategies produce viable architectures
that local reparability strategies cannot?

Explores larger-scale structural consolidation as an alternative to incremental repair.

---
