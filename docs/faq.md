# Frequently Asked Questions

---

## What is AlphaPath?

AlphaPath is a research framework for curriculum architecture analysis, search-based
design exploration, and staged evaluation of professional learning trajectories.

It treats professional curricula as structured architectural objects that can be
represented as graphs, searched, compared, and evaluated — not simply as administrative
subject lists.

---

## What problem does AlphaPath address?

AlphaPath addresses the problem of structural constraint in professional curricula:

- How much of the curriculum's difficulty is architectural rather than pedagogical?
- Do alternative structures exist that reduce that constraint?
- Can those alternatives survive institutional translation?
- Are they robust under uncertainty?

In the Civil Engineering context, AlphaPath was developed in response to structural
evidence that the 2005 curriculum imposes an early and persistent downstream blockage
that cannot be resolved by incremental repair.

---

## What is a PCU?

A Professional Competence Unit (PCU) is the atomic representational unit of AlphaPath.
PCUs are competence-centred building blocks derived from a domain ontology. They replace
subjects as the unit of curricular architecture analysis.

A curriculum represented as a PCU graph is more expressive than a subject list because
it makes progression dependencies, constraint patterns, and structural properties explicit
and measurable.

---

## Is AlphaPath an optimiser?

No. AlphaPath is not an incremental optimiser of existing curriculum plans.

It performs architectural search in the space of PCU-based designs and evaluates
candidates through a staged pipeline. The aim is not to find the best possible curriculum
but to determine whether architectures exist that outperform the baseline under well-defined
evaluation criteria.

---

## Why is the full code not public?

The full AlphaPath codebase depends on institutional data, domain-specific configurations,
and ongoing research pipelines that cannot be publicly released due to confidentiality,
intellectual property, and research-continuity constraints.

This public repository provides the methodological documentation, synthetic examples,
and non-sensitive outputs sufficient for research communication and portfolio purposes.
