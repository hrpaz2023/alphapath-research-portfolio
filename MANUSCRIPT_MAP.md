# Manuscript Map

AlphaPath generated a sequence of research manuscripts and technical papers addressing
different aspects of curriculum architecture analysis and evaluation. This document
provides a public map of that series.

It does not include unpublished drafts or private submission materials.

---

## Core Conceptual Papers

### M1 — AlphaPath: Toward a Search-Based Architecture for Professional Knowledge Navigation

Introduces AlphaPath as a framework for professional learning trajectory discovery.

Develops the shift from subject-based curriculum to competence-centred architecture.
Establishes the PCU representation model, the search-based design paradigm, and the
evaluation stack concept.

**Status:** Public preprint available.
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19552912

---

### M2 — Toward a New Curricular Architecture for Civil Engineering in the Age of Artificial Intelligence

Applies the AlphaPath logic to Civil Engineering education. Develops a taxonomy of
learning in the AI era. Introduces AI-era PCU extensions and compares conservative,
hybrid, and AlphaPath-oriented architectures.

**Status:** Public preprint available.
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19591967

---

## Structural Diagnosis Papers

### M3 — Early-Stage Structural Constraint in the 2005 Civil Engineering Curriculum

Analyses downstream blockage concentration in Modules I–II of the 2005 FACET-UNT
Civil Engineering curriculum. Identifies the forensic floor: the structural constraint
that limits the space of viable redesigns even before institutional translation.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

### M4 — Gateway Compression in Early Modules

Examines gateway concentration and cumulative blockage progression across the early
modules. Identifies support saturation dynamics: the point at which the system exhausts
available support for structurally challenged students.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

## Campaign-Based Evaluation Papers

### M5 — Institutional Translation as a Hard Feasibility Filter

Documents that structural plausibility does not imply institutional viability. Shows
the size of the feasibility gap between structurally valid and institutionally
translatable candidates.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

### M6 — Performance versus Stability under Uncertainty

Distinguishes expected performance from robustness in curriculum evaluation. Develops
the confidence-adjusted return and winner stability metrics. Introduces the claim
discipline principle.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

### M7 — The Limits of Baseline-Near Repair I

Examines local synchronisation relief and prerequisite surgery as redesign strategies.
Establishes the reparation limit: the evidence that incremental structural adjustments
near the baseline do not resolve the underlying architectural constraint.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

### M8 — The Limits of Baseline-Near Repair II

Examines packaging elasticity and subject aggregation as redesign strategies. Extends
the reparation limit analysis to institutional packaging interventions.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.

---

### M9 — Beyond Local Reparability

Synthesises evidence from the campaign series. Argues that the structural constraint
documented in the 2005 curriculum cannot be resolved through incremental redesign and
requires architectural-level intervention.

**Status:** Manuscript in preparation.
