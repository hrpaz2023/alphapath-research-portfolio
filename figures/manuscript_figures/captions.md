# Manuscript Figures — Captions

These three figures are associated with the manuscript series on structural constraint
and reparation limits in the 2005 Civil Engineering curriculum.

---

## Figure 1 — The Forensic Floor

**File:** `Fig_1_Forensic_Floor.png`

**Caption:** Comparative analysis of Plan 2005 graduation rates at nominal duration
(T=10) vs. forensic evaluation window (T=24). The data shows a structural floor where
graduation is effectively zero even under extended time.

**Role in argument:** Establishes the initial structural crisis that necessitates
the search-based repair attempts examined in the campaign series.

---

## Figure 2 — The Persistence Trap

**File:** `Fig_2_Support_Saturation.png`

**Caption:** Visualisation of student persistence (avoidance of non-graduation) vs.
graduation conversion. Highlights how supportive structural overlays rescue students
from dropout but fail to accelerate them toward graduation.

**Role in argument:** Introduces the support saturation concept — the point at which
the system exhausts available structural support for challenged students.

---

## Figure 3 — The Limit of Reparative Search

**File:** `Fig_3_Reparation_Limit.png`

**Caption:** Top feasible set profile of the best-ranking reparative candidates.
Even under optimal heritage-near adjustments and extended time horizons, the graduation
conversion rate peaks at approximately 1.4%.

**Role in argument:** Concludes the empirical evidence for the exhaustion hypothesis —
that the structural constraint documented in Plan 2005 cannot be resolved through
incremental redesign.
